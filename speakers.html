SPEAKERS





LIPIKA SUD

Leading Interior Designer, Entrepreneur and Mentor


Ms. Lipika Sud is a leading interior designer. She is the founder of Lipika Sud Interiors Pvt Ltd. and Dimension Designers Pvt. Ltd. Her unparalleled work earned her the prestigious title of Best Design Professional 2012. Being the president of The Guild of Designers & Artists and an International Executive Council Member of SAARC Chamber Women Entrepreneurship council, she is a woman of great influence and a true inspiration to all. Furthermore, she is passionate about helping humanity grow through the pearls of wisdom that she puts in her YouTube videos.





KAMALNEET SINGH

An educationist, teenage mindset coach, change-preneur, Show Curator: Success Principles by Successful Principals


Mr. Kamalneet Singh is a former chef turned professional in practical psychology and a certified NP practitioner. Mr. Singh has been associated with schools across the nation in different capacities and has trained people from all walks of life and brings with himself a rich experience in coaching people on various aspects of life and has a deep understanding of different levels of interpersonal communication, be it a Student-Teacher relationship, a Parent-Child relationship or workplace communication. A Josh Talks speaker, an avid reader, a change-preneur, he is on a mission to impact one million lives.





ISHA YADAV

Founder - Delhi Art Slam


Isha Yadav-a poet, an artist and a feminist, redefined the slam culture in 2017 with her brainchild ‘Delhi Art Slam’. It is a living and breathing community, as well as the first endeavour in the world to introduce art to the slam culture. She collaborated with ‘The Education Tree’ to generate awareness about mental illness. She is also working on an online expository drive which goes by the name of ‘Museum of Rape Threats & Sexism’, that seeks to bring to light the deep rooted and pernicious attitudes, outlooks, actions and perspectives of the perpetrators.





LOKESH VERMA

International ink artist


Lokesh Verma is an internationally acclaimed veteran Tattoo artist. He is well known across the world for the coloured portrait styled tattoos and sound wave tattoos. He has attained the titles of many ‘firsts’- from being the first Asian to teach tattooing at an Italian University or be it the curation of HeartWork Tattoo Festival- India’s only international tattoo festival. He won the first prize in Inter Asia level painting in Japan. He also holds a Guinness World record for inking maximum number of flags to be tattooed on the human body.





DINESH KANDPAL

Author, Senior Producer – India TV


Mr. Dinesh Kandpal is a senior producer at India TV and a renowned author. His book Parakram, praised by many influential leaders, is the first presentation of the valor of Indian soldiers. He has worked on several well appreciated documentaries. His work earned him a nomination for the Akashvani Annual Award in 1999. During the Kargil War of 1999, he worked on making several programs for All India Radio. He is widely acknowledged for sharing his experience and wisdom with students across various universities. His covering of Delhi Serial Bomb Blast, Anna Andolan, Ramdev's Andolan, Anchoring and Live Reporting of Elections and Budget gave new dimensions to ideas.





SONAL KAPOOR

Human rights activist, Founder- Protsahan India Foundation


Sonal Kapoor is the Founder Director at Protsahan India Foundation - an organization based in New Delhi, India, that works on bridging the gap in childcare systems by introducing an empathy-based system to heal childhood trauma and transform girl children who are either survivors or are at high risk of abuse, to reach their full potential with access to education, healthcare and gender justice. She is an Ashoka Fellow, Vital Voices Fellow & World Bank Fellow. She has pursued Child Protection Law from National Law School of India University and Harvard University. Protsahan, under her leadership, has rescued over 948 girls and reaches out to more than 72,000 vulnerable children across the year in urban slums through arts and technology based mainstreaming programs.
